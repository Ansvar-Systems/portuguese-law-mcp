## Data Authority and Limitations

### Source Authority

This Tool aggregates data from official Portuguese legal databases. However:

| Source Type | Authority Level | Use Case |
|------------|----------------|----------|
| **Official government databases (DRE, PGDL)** | Authoritative | Statute text (primary reference) |
| **Community/open-data sources** | Supplementary | Case law, cross-references (verify independently) |
| **EUR-Lex / treaty databases** | Authoritative for EU/international law | EU law and international obligations metadata |

**CRITICAL**: Community-maintained resources are **NOT** official government publications. While generally reliable, they may contain transcription errors, outdated information, or incomplete coverage.

### Commercial Legal Databases

For professional legal work, **always cross-check with commercial legal databases** such as:

- Base Jurídico-Documental (bdjur), Datajuris, LexisNexis Portugal

These services provide editorial oversight, comprehensive annotations, and professional-grade currency guarantees that this Tool **cannot** provide.

## Currency and Staleness

### No Real-Time Updates

- Database updates are **manual** and may lag official publications by **weeks or months**
- Portuguese law changes continuously through new statutes (leis), decree-laws (decretos-lei), and court decisions (acórdãos)
- **Last-updated timestamps** in tool responses indicate data age, but should be treated as unreliable

### Staleness Warnings

The Tool includes staleness warnings when data is >30 days old. **These warnings are not exhaustive**:

- Even "fresh" data may be outdated if recent amendments were published in the Diário da República
- Staleness warnings do not guarantee completeness or accuracy
- Always check official sources for the current legal position

### Amendments and Repeals

- A provision marked "in force" (em vigor) may have been:
  - Amended (alterado) after the last database update
  - Repealed (revogado) but not yet removed from the database
  - Subject to transitional provisions (disposições transitórias) affecting its applicability
  - Declared unconstitutional by the Tribunal Constitucional
- The Tool does **NOT** automatically track amendments in real-time

## Coverage Gaps

### What's Missing

This Tool does **NOT** include:

1. **Complete case law (jurisprudência)** -- Coverage of Supreme Court (Supremo Tribunal de Justiça), Constitutional Court (Tribunal Constitucional), and lower court decisions is limited
2. **Historical statute versions** -- Limited availability of historical provision wording (versões anteriores)
3. **Legal commentary (doutrina)** -- No editorial annotations, academic commentary, or practice guides
4. **Unpublished decisions** -- Lower court decisions and administrative rulings largely absent
5. **Full legislative history** -- Limited coverage of exposições de motivos and parliamentary debates (debates parlamentares)
6. **Autonomous regions legislation** -- Laws specific to the Azores and Madeira autonomous regions may be incomplete
7. **EU law transposition details** -- Transposition tables and EU directive implementation notes may be incomplete

### Impact on Professional Use

These gaps mean:

- **Incomplete Legal Research**: Tool results are inherently incomplete and may miss critical authorities
- **Context Missing**: Without doutrina and exposições de motivos, interpretation may be incorrect
- **EU Law Integration**: Portugal's EU membership means many provisions implement EU directives; without full transposition context, analysis may be incomplete
- **Tribunal Constitucional Gaps**: Without complete constitutional court rulings, provisions declared unconstitutional may appear as valid
